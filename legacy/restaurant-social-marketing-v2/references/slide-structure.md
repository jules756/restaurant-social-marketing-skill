# Slide Structure & Hook Writing (Restaurant Edition)

## The 6-Slide Formula (EXACTLY 6 — TikTok minimum)

| Slide | Purpose | Text Style |
|-------|---------|------------|
| 1 | HOOK — stop the scroll | Relatable food/dining moment, full hook text |
| 2 | PROBLEM — amplify desire | Build anticipation or tension |
| 3 | DISCOVERY — turning point | "So we walked into this place" / "Then the food arrived..." |
| 4 | TRANSFORMATION 1 — first dish | Reaction: "Wait... this is actually their PASTA??" |
| 5 | TRANSFORMATION 2 — escalate | Reaction: "Okay I'm never cooking at home again" |
| 6 | CTA — call to action | Restaurant name + "link in bio to book" |

**SAME restaurant, SAME lighting vibe, DIFFERENT dishes/angles across all 6 slides.**

## Proven Hook Formulas

### Tier 1: Person + Conflict -> Food -> Changed Mind (BEST)
- "My friend said this place was overrated so I took her there"
- "My boyfriend said he doesn't like [cuisine] until he tried THIS"
- "My mum said restaurant food is a waste of money then she had their [dish]"
- "My colleague said you can't get good [cuisine] in [city] so I proved him wrong"

### Tier 2: FOMO / Discovery
- "I found a restaurant that makes [dish] like this and nobody's talking about it"
- "This hidden gem in [neighborhood] has been there 20 years and most people walk right past it"
- "There's a restaurant in [city] where they [unique thing] and it's insane"
- "The best [dish] I've ever had was from a restaurant you've never heard of"

### Tier 3: Food Reaction / ASMR
- "POV: You walk in and can smell the kitchen from the door"
- "When the waiter brings out the [dish] and the whole table goes silent"
- "This is what [dish] looks like when a chef with 30 years experience makes it"
- "I ordered the [dish] not expecting much but LOOK AT THIS"

### Tier 4: Behind-the-Scenes / Chef
- "POV: The chef who makes your [dish] every night"
- "This is what happens in the kitchen when you order the [signature dish]"
- "3 hours of prep goes into every plate of [dish] and it shows"
- "The secret ingredient in their [dish]? You'd never guess"

### What DOESN'T Work
- Generic food listings without story: "Top 5 dishes at..." (low engagement)
- Price-focused without emotion: "$15 for all this" (feels advertorial)
- Self-promotional: "Come visit our restaurant" (people scroll past)
- Stock-looking food photos without personality or context

## Hook Adaptation by Cuisine

### Italian
- "My nonna would approve of this pasta and she doesn't approve of anything"
- "They make their pasta fresh every morning and you can TASTE the difference"
- "[Restaurant] does a carbonara that would start a fight in Rome"

### Japanese / Sushi
- "This sushi chef trained for 10 years and you can see why"
- "POV: Watching them prepare omakase and trying to stay calm"
- "I've had sushi everywhere but nothing compares to this"

### Mexican
- "Their tacos are so good people drive an hour for them"
- "This is what authentic [dish] looks like when abuela's recipe meets a trained chef"
- "The mole here takes 3 DAYS to make and it hits different"

### Indian
- "My Indian friend said this is the closest to home cooking he's found"
- "They cook everything in a tandoor and the flavour is unreal"
- "I didn't think I liked [dish] until I had it here"

### American / Burgers / BBQ
- "They smoke their brisket for 16 hours and it FALLS APART"
- "This burger is the reason I stopped making them at home"
- "The chef used to work at [fancy place] and now he runs this"

### Fine Dining
- "A 7-course tasting menu that made me forget every meal I've ever had"
- "The attention to detail here is genuinely insane"
- "This is what happens when a chef treats every plate like art"

### Cafe / Brunch
- "Their brunch has a 2-hour wait and now I understand why"
- "I found the perfect coffee spot and I'm gatekeeping it... just kidding"
- "POV: Saturday morning, no plans, and THIS is your brunch"

## Image Prompt Template

Write ONE base description, reuse across all 6 slides:

```
[For AI image gen:]
iPhone photo of [DISH/SCENE] at a [STYLE] restaurant. [DETAILED DESCRIPTION].
Shot from [CAMERA POSITION]. [SPECIFIC TABLE/SETTING DETAILS].
Warm ambient lighting, natural phone camera quality. Portrait orientation.

[SLIDE-SPECIFIC DISH/ANGLE CHANGES ONLY]
```

### What to Lock (same across all 6):
- Table setting (cloth, wood, marble, etc.)
- Plate/dishware style
- Background (exposed brick, window, kitchen pass, etc.)
- Lighting direction and warmth
- Camera angle and distance

### What Changes Per Slide (ONLY):
- The dish / food being shown
- Garnish and plating details
- Small props (wine glass, cutlery position, napkin)
- Steam/sauce/movement

## Caption Template

```
[hook matching slide 1] [2-3 sentences of relatable food story].
So we went to [RESTAURANT NAME] in [location] and honestly??
[emotional reaction to the food]. If you love [cuisine type] you NEED
to try this place. [specific dish recommendation].
Book a table — link in bio
#[cuisine] #[city]food #[neighborhood]eats #foodtiktok #fyp
```

Keep it conversational. Tell a mini-story. Mention the restaurant naturally, not salesy.

## Music (CRITICAL — Do NOT Skip)

Posts are published as **drafts (SELF_ONLY)** to TikTok inbox. Before publishing:

1. Open the draft in TikTok
2. Tap "Add sound" and browse trending sounds — food/cooking sounds work great
3. Pick something popular — trending audio gets algorithmic boost
4. Preview the slideshow with the sound, then publish

**Why drafts?** TikTok's algorithm massively favours posts with trending sounds. Silent slideshows look like ads and get buried. Adding the right music is the difference between 1K and 100K views.

This takes 30 seconds per post. Don't skip it.

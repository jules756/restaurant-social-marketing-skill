# Hook Library (100+ Patterns)

Pick by category. Rewrite in the restaurant's voice. Replace `[dish]`, `[year]`, `[ingredient]`, `[city]`, `[restaurant]`, `[time]` with actual values before posting.

Engagement signal: `HIGH` = consistent top performer across clients. `MED` = reliable mid-tier. `TEST` = worth trying, not a default.

---

## 1. Story-Behind-the-Dish (HIGH — usually #1 category)

*Why it works: specific history + craft signals authenticity. Bookings follow prestige.*

1. `This [dish] has been made the same way since [year].`
2. `My grandmother taught me this recipe. I haven't changed a thing.`
3. `[Year]s of the same [dish]. Here's why it still sells out.`
4. `We stopped changing this recipe in [year]. You'll understand why.`

7. `Made the same way my father made it. The same way his father did.`
8. `[N] years, one recipe. Zero shortcuts.`
9. `People ask us to change this. We never will.`
10. `This recipe doesn't exist anywhere else. Here's why.`

14. `[N] minutes to eat. [N] hours to make.`

## Format Modifiers (Apply to Any Hook Above)

- **Number in the hook** — *"6 hours of prep. 12 minutes to eat."* Numbers beat adjectives.
- **Specific place / year / name** — *"Since 1987"* beats *"for decades"*. Always.
- **First-person singular when possible** — *"I still make it the same way"* beats *"We still make it"*. Humans connect to humans.
- **Short. 6-10 words.** Slide 1 text is read in under a second. Density kills.

## Picking Under Uncertainty

If no memory data and no trend report (cold start), default priority: **Story-Behind-the-Dish → Reaction → Sourcing**. These three work for most restaurants most of the time. Test the others as data accumulates.
